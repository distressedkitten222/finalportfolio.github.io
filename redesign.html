Responsive Redesign of Hopkinton RI Website

Overview

Objective: To conduct a responsive redesign of an existing website with usability issues. This includes analyzing the current interface, creating low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for multiple screen sizes, and building the new responsive website.

Role: Designer, Developer

Webpage to Redesign

I redesigned Hopkinton RI's website, linked here.

I chose this website's homepage because, despite its largely informational and navigational purpose, the information in it is completely unorganized, with no clear layout, hierarchy, or flow.

Usability Evaluation

First, I identified major usability issues, most of which decrease efficiency, learnability, AND memorability:

  • No clear layout or organization of information into sections, so it's difficult for users to find what they want.
  • Crowded text, similarly making it difficult to identify key points.
  • Many redundant and overlapping elements, overwhelming the user without purpose.
    • Multiple menus with overlapping menu items, decreasing efficiency and learnability.
    • Two Departments menus with identical content, decreasing efficiency.
  • UI elements are used in unfamiliar and unintuitive ways, decreasing learnability and memorability.
    • A dropdown menu of 'links' at a random location - a user choosing from this doesn't expect the page to immediately change.
    • Text is underlined but isn't clickable, despite underlined text using indicating a link.
  • Inconsistent links.
    • Links have different fonts, sizes, colours, and styles, decreasing learnability and memorability.
    • Not all links have hover states, so it's easy to miss them - this decreases efficiency, learnability, and memorability.
    • Image links lack labels/alternative text, decreasing learnability as users aren't sure where they lead.
    • One link doesn't have a target, decreasing efficiency.
  • The menu dropdowns have small font, decreasing learnability.
  • The page is not responsive - on smaller screens, the entire page becomes small and difficult to read.

Accessibility Evaluation

Second, I used WebAIM WAVE to detect a number of accessibility issues. Major ones include:

  • A lack of page regions, headings, and structural elements that aid navigation.
  • Links that don't or incorrectly indicate their location.
  • Images without alternative text, including linked images.

The accessibility issues detected follow a major theme of the usability issues - difficulty navigating, both in the page and to other pages. It seems that the lack of structure and consistency is an issue not just visually but also for screen readers, making the page quite inaccessible in multiple ways.

Learning

This project taught me a lot, but I won't make you read all of what I learned. These are my main takeaways:

  • Seemingly small changes can have drastic effects on an interface's usability - things as small as underlining vs. not underlining text.
  • I can make a responsive website now! :D

Next Steps
